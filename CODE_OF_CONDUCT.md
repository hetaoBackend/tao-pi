# Code of Conduct

TaoPi is meant to be a welcoming, practical place to learn, build, and discuss
agent tooling. We value clear communication, patience, curiosity, and respect
for different backgrounds and levels of experience.

## Expected Behavior

- Be kind, direct, and constructive.
- Assume good intent, while taking responsibility for your own impact.
- Give feedback about ideas and code, not personal traits.
- Make room for beginners and for people who communicate differently from you.
- Respect privacy. Do not publish private conversations, credentials, personal
  data, or sensitive project information without permission.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable in project spaces:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or sustained disruption.
- Insults, slurs, sexualized attention, or demeaning comments.
- Public or private abuse based on identity, background, experience level, or
  personal circumstances.
- Sharing private information without explicit consent.
- Deliberately derailing discussions, reviews, or issue threads.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in all TaoPi project spaces, including issues,
pull requests, discussions, documentation, chat channels, events, and any public
representation of the project.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, contact the project
maintainers through the repository's normal private or public reporting channel.
If no private channel is available, open an issue with only the minimum detail
needed to request maintainer contact.

Maintainers should handle reports respectfully, protect privacy where possible,
and avoid unnecessary disclosure.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they consider appropriate, including:

- asking for clarification or an apology
- editing or removing comments, commits, issues, or pull requests
- temporarily limiting participation
- permanently banning a participant from project spaces

Enforcement decisions should be guided by the safety of the community, the
severity and pattern of behavior, and the willingness of the participant to
repair harm.
